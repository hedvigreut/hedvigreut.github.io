ABOUT THE PROJECT

As part of my visualisation track in my master Interactive technology, me and my group created a mobile game in the module DH2560 Computer Game Design.

We wanted to create a game with animals that covered an engaging topic that was up-to-date. Personally, the environment is an important subject. We were all concerned with the growing effect of the emissions of greenhouse gases, and in particular the meat industry which has a variety of effects on the Earth. Through fossil fuel usage and chemical compounds and waste, it pollutes the air and the water. It also puts stress on aquatic eco systems and wildlife, and is fuels antibiotic resistance as well as being the largest source globally of greenhouse gas emissions.

Why not save these animals instead of eating them?

This is how we came up with the idea of the game of trying to save livestock from being eaten by people.

The player is breaking into slaughterhouses and saving animals from slaughter by building conveyer belts to getting the animals safety, dodging sharp blades while heading for the exit.

I was the lead designer, designing the logo, the graphical profile, backgrounds, elements and animals. We used Unity to develop the game and MagicaVoxel for building models. I made all designs through MagicaVoxel, Adobe Photoshop and Illustrator.
